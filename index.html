Miles Morales

Meet Miles Morales, the sensational Spider-Man from an alternate universe. Bursting onto the scene in the comic world in 2011, Miles brings a fresh perspective to the Spidey legacy. Hailing from Brooklyn, this Afro-Latino teen not only swings between skyscrapers but also navigates the challenges of being a high school student. His unique powers, including the ability to camouflage and administer a venomous shock, make Miles a standout Spider-Man in the multiverse.

Tobey Maguire

In the early 2000s, Tobey Maguire spun his way into the hearts of audiences as the original big-screen Spider-Man. Sam Raimi's trilogy portrayed Peter Parker's journey from an awkward teen to a seasoned hero. Maguire's portrayal is nostalgic for many, capturing the essence of the character's enduring spirit and responsibility. Tobey's Spidey is a classic, and his web-slinging adventures set the standard for the superhero genre.

Andrew Garfield

Enter Andrew Garfield, the Amazing Spider-Man of the 2010s. Garfield brought a different energy to Peter Parker, infusing the character with wit and a skateboarding coolness. His journey explored the untold story of Peter's parents and introduced a Gwen Stacy romance. Though the franchise faced challenges, Garfield's charismatic performance earned him a place in the Spider-Verse, showcasing a Spider-Man dealing with love, loss, and a quest for truth.

Tom Holland

The latest addition to the Spider-Verse, Tom Holland swung into action, seamlessly blending the awkwardness of adolescence with the heroics of Spider-Man. As part of the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), Holland's Spider-Man navigates a world filled with Avengers and cosmic threats. His youthful exuberance, quick quips, and mentorship from Tony Stark make Tom's Spidey a unique and beloved iteration, weaving seamlessly into the larger tapestry of Marvel's cinematic universe.
